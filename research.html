Welcome to the Research section of IRFF Genome Institute. We are committed to advancing scientific knowledge across various domains. Please explore our research areas below to learn more about our specific projects and initiatives.

Genetic Disorders

Drug Discovery for Neurodegenerative Diseases

Developing innovative therapeutic drugs targeting neurodegenerative conditions like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.

Rett Syndrome Research

Researching new drugs to treat Rett Syndrome, focusing on genetic and molecular interventions.

Leigh Syndrome Research

Investigating genetic mutations and metabolic pathways in Leigh Syndrome to develop effective therapies.

Immunology

Immunological Research (NK Cells)

Exploring the role of Natural Killer cells in combating viruses and cancer for enhanced immune therapies.

Neuroscience

New Tools for Behavioral Neuroscience

Developing AI-driven tools to track and analyze behavior in model organisms.

Brain Fatigue Research (BOOCS)

The BOOCS (a novel concept developed by professor Takehiko Fujino) could potentially rescue Brain Fatigue.

Genomics and Bioinformatics

Exploring New Tools for Genomics

Developing cutting-edge tools and methodologies for advanced genomic research.

Genome Editing (CRISPR-Cas9) Technology

Utilizing CRISPR-Cas9 for precise genome editing to study genetic functions and therapies.

Transcriptomics Analysis Softwares

Creating advanced software tools for comprehensive gene expression and interaction analysis.

Lipidomics

Lipidomics

Studying plasmalogens as biomarkers for Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and aging using advanced techniques.

Plasmalogen Extraction

Our Institute Developed nad Patented Plasmalogens Extraction Methods for use in Clinical Trials and Use as Supplements.

Anti-Aging and Metabolism

Anti-Aging Research (C. elegans)

Exploring genetic and molecular mechanisms influencing longevity using C. elegans as a model organism.

Molecular Dynamics and Simulation for New Drug Discovery

Utilizing simulation techniques to model interactions between drug compounds and biological targets.
